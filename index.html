The Great Mauritian Dodo

About

The Dodo is an extinct flightless bird that originated from the Island of Mauritius east of Madagascar. These birds are most well-known for their ability to not fly and large hooked beaks. The color scheme of the Dodo included being pale yellow or green. Scientist have linked the Nicobar pigeon to being the closest relative to the Dodo.

Appearance

The Dodo was a pretty heavy bird comparing to birds today. It weighed about 23-47 pounds and was roughly 3 feet and 3 inches tall. There is no picture of the dodo from real life encounters. All the appearance of the Dodo have been drawn, painted and written accounts from scientists from the 17th century.

Behavior

There is not much recorded about the behavior of the Dodo bird. It has been said in journals from the 17th century as having brownish grey plumage, yellow feet, a tuft of tail feathers, a grey, naked head, and a black -yellow and green beak.

Physical Remains

The only existing remains of the Dodo was collected in the 17th century. The remains are scattered all over the world. Different parts of the Dodo range to be anywhere from Universities to museums.
